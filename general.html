What Is Misinformation?

Misinformation: “false or inaccurate information, especially that which is deliberately intended to deceive.”

Misinformation has plagued the world long before it was even a part of anyone’s vocabulary. Governments, social media influencers and many workplaces have been targeted with misinformation and it is easily spread due to the nature of the online world.

How To Avoid and Identify Misinformation?

- Many pieces of information can be easy fact-checked with a google search
- Spelling/Grammatical error can indicate unreliable information
- Check the source, are they reliable, does the publisher or journalist have a history of bias
- Why was it sent? Could there be alternative biases for writing the piece?
Check for other sources reporting the same news, cross-check multiple sources. Do these other - sources approve or disapprove of the original source?

- Don’t rely on social media for important news
- Pay extra attention to emotionally charged topics
- Investigate what you are reading
